Sapporo ramen

Origin: Hokkaido, Japan Broth: Miso-based, rich and slightly sweet Noodles: Thick, curly, and chewy Toppings: Corn, butter, chashu pork, bean sprouts, bamboo shoots, green onions Description: Sapporo Ramen is a famous Hokkaido-style ramen known for its bold miso-flavored broth and hearty toppings. It was created in the 1950s in Sapporo, the capital of Hokkaido, where the cold climate influenced the development of a warm, filling ramen perfect for winter. The broth is made from miso paste, pork bones, and chicken stock, giving it a deep umami taste with a slightly nutty, savory-sweet flavor. The signature thick, curly noodles are perfect for absorbing the rich broth. Unique Features: Corn & Butter: A Hokkaido specialty, adding sweetness and creaminess Wok-fried Ingredients: Some restaurants fry garlic, onions, and miso paste before adding broth, enhancing flavor Topped with Chashu Pork: Slow-braised pork belly adds richness Sapporo Ramen is best enjoyed hot, and some variations include spicy miso or seafood-based miso broths, making it a must-try for ramen lovers! Onomichi ramen

Origin: Onomichi, Hiroshima Prefecture, Japan Broth: Shoyu (soy sauce) with pork and seafood flavors Noodles: Flat, firm, and slightly chewy Toppings: Chashu pork, green onions, bamboo shoots, pork back fat Description: Onomichi Ramen is a distinctive soy sauce-based ramen that originates from Onomichi, a coastal city in Hiroshima Prefecture. It features a light yet deeply umami broth that blends chicken, pork bones, and small dried sardines (Niboshi) to create a savory, slightly briny flavor. What makes Onomichi Ramen stand out is the generous topping of pork back fat (seabura) floating on the broth. This gives the ramen a rich mouthfeel while keeping the soup itself relatively clear and light. Unique Features: Pork Back Fat (Seabura): Adds richness and enhances the broth‚Äôs depth without making it too heavy. Flat, Firm Noodles: These noodles have a great chewiness, absorbing the broth while maintaining texture. Seafood-Infused Broth: Unlike typical shoyu ramen, Onomichi Ramen has a distinct taste from dried sardines, adding a slightly salty umami layer. Serving Style: Onomichi Ramen is usually topped with thinly sliced chashu, green onions, bamboo shoots, and a soft-boiled egg. Some variations include spicy chili paste or extra garlic for added complexity. This ramen is especially popular among those who love soy sauce-based broths but want an extra touch of richness.
